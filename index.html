Website Modernization Proposal

Prepared for Public Utility District No. 1 of Pend Oreille County  |  RFP 26-508  |  July 2026

Dear Members of the Pend Oreille PUD Selection Committee,

Your website is a service counter — and it's open when the counter isn't. When a customer needs to pay a bill at 9 p.m., report an outage from a phone in a low-signal corner of the county, or figure out which of your four systems they belong to, popud.org is the staff member who answers. That's the project we're proposing to build: not a brochure, but the front counter for a utility that has been serving this county since 1936.

Our approach is defined by two commitments. The first is a methodology rooted in evidence, not guesswork. We use specialized user-path mapping, customer task analysis, and behavioral research — including webcam eye-tracking studies of how ordinary people actually read tiered pricing — to eliminate navigational friction and build clear, direct pathways for residential customers, commercial and industrial customers, and the community members, job seekers, and regulatory bodies who come to the District for public information.

The second is being your most intentional partner. We don't act as an outside vendor; we integrate into your team. That includes traveling to Newport at our own cost for the project kickoff, and returning on a committed cadence throughout the engagement.

You'll also find us unusually direct in this proposal — about what we're actually proposing for SmartHub, about certifications we do and don't hold, and about the limits of what any vendor can honestly promise on legal compliance. We'd rather tell you exactly what we deliver than write confident language around what we can't.

We certify that we have carefully examined all RFP documents, fully understand your objectives, and are confident in delivering a modern, service-oriented digital experience for customers across Pend Oreille County.

We look forward to hearing from you,

How It Works

Six phases.
24 weeks to launch.

01
Discovery & Planning
We travel to Newport for an in-person kickoff at no cost to the District — meeting leadership, commissioners, and the staff who answer the phones. Stakeholder interviews across all four systems, customer task analysis, and a full content and performance audit produce a recommended sitemap and visual strategy for your approval before anything gets built.
02
Design & Wireframing
Figma wireframes map the structure and navigation, then high-fidelity mockups show exactly how the site will look on desktop, tablet, and mobile. Plain-language copywriting runs in parallel, and a WCAG 2.1 AA design audit catches contrast, hierarchy, and focus-order problems while they’re still cheap to fix.
03
Development
A WordPress core with Elementor Pro, extended with custom code — a bespoke frontend for your customers and a drag-and-drop backend for your staff. This phase delivers the CMS and editorial roles, alert banner, search with filtering, document library, careers hub, rate presentation, content migration, and a full 301 redirect map.
04
Implementation & Testing
SmartHub connection, fillable and e-signable forms, and analytics are completed and tested in staging. Multi-device QA, cross-browser checks, comprehensive accessibility testing, security hardening, and two to three rounds of District revisions against a staging beta your team can walk through directly.
05
Launch & Training
We deploy to popud.org during a low-traffic window — database migration, SSL, redirects, sitemap submission — then run hands-on training built for non-technical staff, and deliver a recorded, task-organized video library plus written architecture and maintenance documentation.
06
Post-Launch Optimization
Ninety days of evidence-based refinement. Analytics and heatmap review, follow-up customer interviews, and design and technical adjustments based on how your community actually uses the live site — backed by a reserved $7,500 optimization fund.

Your review gates

We name your review points explicitly rather than implying them, so your staff and commissioners can plan around them: information architecture and sitemap review at the end of Phase 1; formal design approval at the end of Phase 2; a staging beta walkthrough in Phase 4; and final launch sign-off in Phase 5. Phase 1 runs four weeks because four separately financed systems and three elected commissioners are more stakeholder surface than a single-service organization — and Phase 3 absorbs the Thanksgiving-through-New-Year period honestly rather than pretending it won’t affect either team.

The Investment

Transparent,
ratepayer-minded

We are conscious that every dollar in this proposal is a ratepayer dollar that three elected commissioners will be asked to account for in a public meeting — and we have structured our pricing accordingly. This is a fixed-fee, not-to-exceed investment of $75,900 for the complete required scope, with all contingency included. Preferred and future-focused capabilities are priced separately below, so the District decides what to add, and when, with full visibility into what each item costs.

Every dollar is tied directly to a defined project phase and milestone. Rates follow our standard card: $250/hr for strategy, research, design, development, and copywriting; $150/hr for production design; $100/hr for meetings and project coordination. Milestone billing: 25% at signing, 25% at design approval, 25% at staging beta, 25% at launch.
